On the way to the grocery store, I walk through a park. It's a lovely little park with fountains, dogs and flowers, and by the time I get to the grocery store the charm of the walk usually makes my shopping happy and painless (except when they are out of eggs--I mean seriously, what grocery store runs out of eggs?).



I believe good design is like my park. Good design makes any menial task pleasant and rewarding. Good design is what drives me to research, problem solve, and create.



I am always looking for new design challenges and opportunities. I am currently seeking full or part-time work in UX design or research. Reach out directly if you'd like to work with me or learn more about my passion for analytics, visuals, and strategy!

It has always been hard to answer the "where are you from" question. Short story: I guess Tuscaloosa, Alabama? Long story: I was born in New Orleans, moved to North Carolina when I was 1, moved to San Francisco when I was 3, then moved to Austin TX when I was 5. Along the way, my mom and I picked up a Dad and a couple of extra siblings, camped out in Moscow for a year, and finally relocated to Tuscaloosa, Alabama for my high school years.



After that I moved to Charlottesville, Virginia for 3 years to attend the University of Virginia. I graduated with a double major in Media Studies and Women, Gender, Sexuality Studies in May of 2016. I threw myself into tour guiding with the University Guide Service, event management with the University Programs Council, and moderating for Sustained Dialogue. I loved my time at UVa and will miss those free Hoos basketball tickets until the day I die.



The summer of '16 I moved to Washington D.C. (as many a UVa grad is want to do) and started working for Clutch, a B2B start-up here in the city. As a Content Developer and Marketer my primary role is crafting and analysing surveys, writing up articles and blog posts, and pitching and publishing our content. I have also taken on a large role in the company's web design and graphic design process through wireframing and data visualization on our site.

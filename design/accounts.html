Accounts Management

Through stakeholder research, discovery interviews, and usability tests, I built a new account management and onboarding experience for Morning Consult's SAAS product.

TLDR: Morning Consult, a survey research and polling company, wanted to bring survey fielding in-house. As the design owner, I was responsible for research, user experience, and execution.

  • Role: Lead Product Designer
  • Software Used: Figma, UserTesting, Amplitude, Miro, Jira
  • Shipped product
  • -50% tech support tickets
Details
User Problem
Marriott was mad that they couldn’t share content with each other. Account management was notoriously unreliable and caused endless support tickets. Basic functionality (reset user password, reset user email, invite many new users, assign data access) was missing. How did we find out about these problems?

Stakeholder research
Technically, all content was tethered to a single user account that had no concept of teams. This meant that there was no way for us to allow for content to pass between accounts without risking a serious data breach. The old tech stack prevented us from implementing important security measures and made developing new features painful.

User flow & wireframes
Make a new user flow that includes creating a new team and sharing a dashboard link with their teammates. The wireframes were heavily informed by the developers on my team. It was a collaborative effort to define the MVP.
What did I do to get these wireframes approved? How did they change? These wireframes helped secure the resources of 2 scrum teams to get this work done.

The solution
The MVP solution for accounts encompassed all of the existing jobs to be done, and introduced the most requested features from users. It also reconfigured the database to recognize different client teams and a toggle to switch between them. Link sharing was the lowest lift option to satisfy user sharing needs.

Pilot release
Because this change would affect the day-to-day lives of 200+ users, we rolled out the new section with a handful of pilot users before moving onto a wider release. This gave us a chance to kick the tires and fix any bugs we found as well as refining our release documentation to best communicate these changes.
